About the converter

This tool follows the official 1995 Uzbek Latin alphabet. It recognizes digraphs such as sh → ш, ch → ч, ng → нг, oʻ → ў, and gʻ → ғ. In Cyrillic to Latin conversion, е becomes ye at the start of a word and after vowels, and e elsewhere.

Text is converted entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to the server.

Frequently asked questions

Which alphabet standard does the converter use?

It follows the official 1995 Uzbek Latin alphabet standard, the version used in schools and official documents.

Is the apostrophe handled correctly?

Sanʼat → санъат, maʼno → маъно, taʼlim → таълим. The apostrophe is rendered as ъ in Cyrillic. Common apostrophe variants are accepted.

How many words can I convert?

You can convert up to 5,000 words at once, roughly a 10-15 page document.

Are my texts stored?

No. Conversion happens entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to the server.

How is the Cyrillic Е converted?

Cyrillic → Latin: at the start of a word and after vowels it becomes ye; elsewhere it becomes e. For example: ечмоқ → yechmoq, but бекор → bekor.

Does uppercase text work correctly?

Yes. TOSHKENT → ТОШКЕНТ, Toshkent → Тошкент, and toshkent → тошкент are handled correctly.

About Latin ↔ Cyrillic conversion

Uzbek switched to the Latin script in the 1990s, but Cyrillic is still widely used in older documents, books, and institutions. This tool converts between both scripts instantly.

Conversion rules

The Uzbek Latin alphabet has 29 letters. Special letters such as oʻ and gʻ, plus their Cyrillic equivalents, are converted correctly.

How to use it?

  1. Enter or paste Uzbek text in Latin or Cyrillic into the input field.
  2. Choose Latin → Cyrillic or Cyrillic → Latin.
  3. Use the Copy button to copy the result to your clipboard.
Are all Uzbek letters supported?

Yes. oʻ, gʻ, sh, ch, ng, yo, yu, ya and their Cyrillic equivalents are supported.

How much text can I enter?

The interface is optimized for up to 5,000 words per conversion, which is enough for most documents.

Is text sent to the server?

No. Conversion is performed locally in your browser with JavaScript.
