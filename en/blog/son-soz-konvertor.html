Number → Words Converter: Rules for Writing Numbers as Text in Uzbek

Financial documents require numbers to be written both as digits and as words. Making mistakes in Uzbek is easy without knowing the rules — a complete guide and free tool.

1. Why write numbers as words?

In financial documents — cheques, contracts, receipts and bank documents — the amount must be written both as digits and as words. This prevents document fraud: altering digits is easy, but changing a word-written amount is visible and difficult.

In Uzbekistan, the Tax Code and Central Bank guidelines make it mandatory to write amounts as words in financial documents.

2. Basic rules for writing numbers in Uzbek

2.1. Units (0–9)

  • 0 → nol  |  1 → bir  |  2 → ikki  |  3 → uch  |  4 → to’rt
  • 5 → besh  |  6 → olti  |  7 → yetti  |  8 → sakkiz  |  9 → to’qqiz

2.2. Tens (10–99)

  • 10 → o’n  |  20 → yigirma  |  30 → o’ttiz  |  40 → qirq  |  50 → ellik
  • 60 → oltmish  |  70 → yetmish  |  80 → sakson  |  90 → to’qson

Tens and units are separated by a space: 25 → yigirma besh, 73 → yetmish uch.

2.3. Hundreds (100–999)

  • 100 → bir yuz  |  200 → ikki yuz  |  500 → besh yuz
  • 342 → uch yuz qirq ikki  |  999 → to’qqiz yuz to’qson to’qqiz

2.4. Thousands, millions, billions

  • 1 000 → bir ming  |  10 000 → o’n ming  |  100 000 → yuz ming
  • 1 000 000 → bir million  |  1 000 000 000 → bir milliard

Example: 2 500 000 so’m → ikki million besh yuz ming so’m

💡 Important rule: All groups are separated only by a space when written as words — no comma or dash. “Ikki million besh yuz ming”, not “ikki million, besh yuz ming”.

3. Correct format for financial documents

Correct form for a contract or cheque:

“Total amount: 3 750 000 (uch million yetti yuz ellik ming) so’m.”

“Payment amount: 125 500 (yuz yigirma besh ming besh yuz) so’m 00 tiyin.”

4. Common mistakes

  • 40 → “qirik” instead of the correct “qirq”.
  • 1000 → “mingta” instead of “bir ming” — in financial documents “bir ming” is correct.
  • 20 → “yigirmi” instead of the standard “yigirma”.
  • Comma-separated groups: “ikki million, besh yuz ming” → should be “ikki million besh yuz ming”.
  • Decimals: 1.5 → “bir butun beshdan bir” or “bir yarim”.

5. The risk of writing large numbers by hand

6. Tiyin and decimals

In Uzbekistan, so’m is divided into tiyin (1 so’m = 100 tiyin). In financial documents:

  • 150 500.75 → “bir yuz ellik ming besh yuz so’m yetmish besh tiyin”
  • 1 000.00 → “bir ming so’m nol tiyin” or simply “bir ming so’m”

In practice, tiyin is rarely used — most documents list only whole so’m amounts.
