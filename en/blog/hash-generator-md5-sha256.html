MD5 & SHA-256 Hash Generator Explained

Discover how MD5 and SHA-256 hashes work and generate them online for verification and testing.

2026-05-03 5 min

What is a Hash?

A hash is a fixed-length fingerprint generated from input data. MD5 and SHA-256 are common hashing algorithms used for checksums and security.

How to Use the Tool

Paste your text into the generator, choose the algorithm, and copy the result. The tool works entirely in your browser without uploading data.

When to Use MD5 vs SHA-256

Use MD5 for quick checksums and non-security tasks. Use SHA-256 when you need a stronger, collision-resistant hash.

Frequently asked questions

Can hashes be reversed?

No, hashes are designed to be one-way functions.

Is hashing secure?

SHA-256 is secure for checksums, while MD5 is suitable for compatibility and non-sensitive uses.
