Unit Conversion: km, mile, kg, lb, °C, °F — Formulas and Examples

A practical conversion reference for travel, cooking, sport and everyday life.

When foreign websites show prices in pounds, temperatures in Fahrenheit and distances in miles — you need conversion knowledge. This guide collects the most-used measurement units and their formulas.

Length: km, mile, meter, inch

Most of the world uses the metric system (km, m, cm). The US, UK and a few others use the imperial system (mile, foot, inch).

Temperature: Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin

Temperature conversion is the trickiest — it is not a simple multiplication; an offset is added too.

ConversionFormulaExample
°C → °F(x × 9/5) + 3237°C = 98.6°F
°F → °C(x − 32) × 5/998.6°F = 37°C
°C → Kx + 273.150°C = 273.15 K
K → °Cx − 273.15300 K = 26.85°C

Key points:

  • Water freezes: 0°C = 32°F
  • Normal body temperature: 37°C = 98.6°F
  • Water boils: 100°C = 212°F
  • Cold winter: −10°C = 14°F
  • Hot summer: 40°C = 104°F

FAQ

How many km in a mile? 1 mile = 1.60934 km (≈ 1.6 km).

37°C in Fahrenheit? (37 × 9/5) + 32 = 98.6°F.

Litres in a gallon? US gallon = 3.785 L. UK gallon = 4.546 L.

Kg in a pound? 1 lb = 0.4536 kg. 100 lb ≈ 45.4 kg.

Cm in an inch? 1 in = 2.54 cm.
