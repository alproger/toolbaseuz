Deleting PDF Pages: Detailed Guide

Methods for removing unwanted pages from PDF documents — visual selection and page range input.

1. Why delete pages?

PDFs you receive often contain extra pages: blank pages, cover sheets, advertising pages or accidental scans. Removing them gives you a clean document with only the pages you need.

The most common scenarios:

  • Extracting a specific section from a multi-page report
  • Removing blank pages from a scanned document
  • Deleting certain topics from a textbook or book
  • Removing confidential pages before sharing a document publicly

2. Two selection methods

The PDF Page Delete tool on toolbase.uz offers two approaches:

Visual

🖰 Click to select

You see a thumbnail of each page. Click the pages you want to delete — they turn red with a delete indicator.

Range

🔢 Enter a range

Type something like “5 to 12” and those pages are automatically selected.

💡 For long documents the range method is much faster. For example, deleting pages 50–100 from a 200-page book is far quicker by typing a range than clicking each thumbnail.

1

3

4

4. Range selection — step by step

1
Upload your PDF file.
2
Click the “Range input” tab.
3
Enter “From” and “To” page numbers. For example, 5 to 10 selects pages 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 and 10.
4
Click “Select” — pages are highlighted automatically. Add more ranges if needed.
5
Click “Delete selected pages” and download.

5. Important limitations

  • At least 1 page must remain — you cannot delete all pages.
  • Password-protected PDFs cannot be processed — remove the password first.
  • The tool works in the browser, so an internet connection is required (libraries are loaded from a CDN).

⚠️ Warning: Deleted pages cannot be recovered. Always keep a backup of the original file before deleting.

6. Deleting pages vs splitting (extract)

Sometimes you want to extract a section rather than delete pages. For example, extracting pages 20–30 from a 50-page document as a separate file. In that case:

  • Delete pages 1–19 and 31–50 → pages 20–30 remain
  • Or create a new PDF with just the pages you need (a split feature is coming soon)
