Uzbek Spell Check: Apostrophe, Grammar and Common Mistakes

The most common spelling mistakes in Uzbek Latin script: words with apostrophes, capitalisation rules, punctuation — and how to correct them.

Most common spelling mistakes in Uzbek

Uzbek switched to Latin script in 1993. Since then, many people make mistakes with apostrophes, letter substitutions and punctuation. Below are the most widespread problems and their correct forms.

1. The apostrophe — the most frequent source of error

In Uzbek Latin script, the apostrophe (‘) represents two sounds:

  • O’ (rounded o): o’yin, o’qituvchi, o’g’il, ko’z
  • G’ (ghayn): g’oya, g’alaba, tog’, bag’ri

Common mistake: Many people write a plain apostrophe (') instead of o’, or omit it entirely: oyin, oquvchi. This is orthographically incorrect.

Correct vs incorrect examples

  • ✓ o’yin  ✗ oyin, o’yin
  • ✓ g’oya  ✗ goya
  • ✓ ko’cha  ✗ kocha
  • ✓ to’g’ri  ✗ togri
  • ✓ o’qituvchi  ✗ oquvchi

Technical note: In Unicode, the Uzbek apostrophe can be U+2018 (LEFT SINGLE QUOTATION MARK) or U+02BC (MODIFIER LETTER APOSTROPHE). Many apps also accept the plain apostrophe U+0027.

2. C/Ts and X/Kh confusion

Some letters in the Uzbek Latin alphabet confuse new learners:

  • C — represents the “ts” sound in Uzbek, not “j”: tsement
  • X — the hard “kh” sound: xona, xat, xalq
  • Ch — two letters together for the “ch” sound: choy, chiroq
  • Sh — two letters together for the “sh” sound: shahar, shon

3. Capitalisation rules

In Uzbek, a capital letter is used:

  • At the start of a sentence.
  • Personal names: Alisher Navoiy, Zahiriddin Muhammad Bobur
  • Geographical names: Toshkent, O’zbekiston, Amudaryo
  • Organisation names: Oliy Majlis, Milliy bank
  • Holidays: Navro’z bayrami, Mustaqillik kuni

Common mistake: Titles like “president”, “doktor”, “professor” are written in lower case. However “O’zbekiston Respublikasi Prezidenti” — as an official title — takes a capital letter.

4. Punctuation

Comma (,)

  • Between listed items: olma, nok, uzum
  • Between clauses in a compound sentence
  • Around parenthetical words or phrases: Albatta, bu to’g’ri.

Semicolon (;)

Between related independent clauses when a stronger pause than a comma is needed: Kecha ko’p ish qildim; bugun dam olaman.

Colon (:)

Before an explanation, a list or direct speech: Ikki narsa kerak: sabr va mehnat.
